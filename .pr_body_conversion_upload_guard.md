## Summary

- add an early `Content-Length` guard for conversion uploads that are clearly above the supported `10 MB` ceiling
- instrument conversion checkpoints so logs show whether a request died during upload read, PDF inspection, analyze start, OCR start, or result persistence
- persist a `Processando` attempt before heavy conversion work and update the same record to `Sucesso` or `Falha` for user and anonymous flows
- update conversion test doubles and focused coverage to prove the preallocated attempt id is reused instead of creating duplicate history rows

## Why

Production restarts were interrupting PDF-to-OFX attempts before the app could emit a final SSE event or persist failure telemetry. We needed two safeguards: reject obviously oversized uploads earlier, and leave a durable trace that the attempt existed even if the process dies during parsing or OCR.

- [ ] Requires manual operation

The schema is unchanged, but conversion history behavior changes:

- `user_conversions` and `anonymous_conversion_events` now receive an early `Processando` row before the heavy analyze step
- the same row is updated to `Sucesso` or `Falha` when the request finishes normally
- if the process dies mid-flight, the leftover `Processando` row becomes the durable signal that the attempt started
